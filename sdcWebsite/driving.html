2.1 Image processing part

The goal of the image processing is to detect lanes in real-time. And it consists of the following steps.

Step 1: Find edges with Canny Edge Detection

Step 2: Divide the image into five sections

By dividing the image into five sections, it enables us to distinguish between straight and curved roads. The dark blue and light blue represent lane boundaries from different sections. And the lane boundaries in each section intersect at certain point, a and b. In the image below on the left, a and b are almost the same. The reason is pretty obvious, on a straight road, the lane boundary segment all lie on the same line, so they always intersect at the same point. While in the image below on the right, since its a curved road, lane boundaries intersect at different points a and b because of the curvature. These different patterns tell us the road shape. Note that further image processing all takes place in each section independently. And we only care about the second and third section counting from the top, because change in road shapes in these two sections is enough for us to predict curves.
